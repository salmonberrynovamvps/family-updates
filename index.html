Ledford Family Update

Last updated: April 28, 2026
Current Status
We are still in our home. The earliest unlawful detainer (formal eviction) filing window opened April 27 — as of April 28, nothing has been filed. We have an attorney retained (Austin Hatcher), and we're activating a backup eviction defense channel (Northwest Justice Project hotline) this week as a safety net while Austin is briefly out of office. New volunteers — including a JAG attorney and a peer-support advocate with LA-area veteran-services connections — joined the campaign this week. Multiple federal and state investigations remain open. We are preparing for both outcomes — staying and relocating — and have a plan for each.
Important context

"Thousands of veterans who took a forbearance are now at risk of losing their homes through no fault of their own." — NPR, The VA Loan Calamity

This was not a matter of missed payments, negligence, or irresponsibility. We made every call, submitted every application, followed up on every deadline, and did everything we were told to do — often doing the servicer's job for them. The VA and our mortgage servicer (Freedom Mortgage, a designated "repeat offender" with $130.3 million in federal penalties) put us into a cycle of programs that were cancelled, restarted, and stalled for years. None of it mattered, because Freedom Mortgage wasn't submitting the paperwork. This was confirmed on a recorded call.

What happened to our family is part of a documented, systemic failure — not something we caused or could have prevented. NPR, Congress, the VA Inspector General, and major veteran service organizations are all actively investigating or advocating on this crisis. For the full background, see the bottom of this page.

Background

If you're new to this or want to understand how we got here, here's the full picture.

This isn't just about our family. Since the VA killed their rescue program (VASP) in May 2025, over 10,000 veteran families have already lost their homes. Another 90,000 are currently at risk of foreclosure right now. The total number of impacted families is over 160,000 and climbing. NPR, Rolling Stone, the Rachel Maddow Show, Congress, the VA Inspector General, and major veteran service organizations have all been investigating and advocating on this crisis.

Our mortgage servicer, Freedom Mortgage, has $130.3 million in cumulative federal penalties. They've been fined by the CFPB in 2019, 2023, and 2024. The Department of Justice hit them with a $113 million settlement for certifying loans that didn't meet standards. New Jersey's Attorney General issued a consent order for "bait-and-switch" refinancing targeting veterans. This is who the VA entrusted with our loan.

We were not asking for a handout. We were asking to pay our mortgage. We were not allowed to. Freedom Mortgage and the VA put us into a cycle of programs that were cancelled, restarted, and stalled — for years. We did everything we were told to do. We made every call, submitted every application, followed up on every deadline. None of it mattered, because Freedom Mortgage wasn't submitting the paperwork. This was confirmed on a recorded call.

We found out about the foreclosure when someone showed up at our door with the notice.

The documented violations: Freedom Mortgage offered us a 7.25% interest rate on our 2.25% VA loan — that's more than triple our rate, and 400 basis points over the VA's own cap. They promised a VASP review in writing in January 2025, then marked every single option "Not Reviewed" 19 days later. The VA foreclosed while the loss mitigation application sat unsubmitted. There are nine documented regulatory violations in our case, with active investigations at CFPB, VA OIG, and the WA Attorney General.

There is an active congressional investigation. The VA Inspector General has an open federal investigation. We have the evidence, the documentation, and the support of multiple congressional offices and organizations — and now a retained attorney. What we still don't have is our home secured — because the VA has the authority to fix this and won't.

For the full series that started this: NPR — The VA Loan Calamity

We'll keep updating this page as things develop. We're exhausted but we're still in it.
