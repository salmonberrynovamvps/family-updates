Ledford Family Update

Last updated: April 9, 2026
Current Status
We are still in our home. No eviction has been filed yet. Multiple organizations and congressional offices are actively working on our case. We may still need to relocate — we're preparing for both outcomes.
Important context

"Thousands of veterans who took a forbearance are now at risk of losing their homes through no fault of their own." — NPR, The VA Loan Calamity

This was not a matter of missed payments, negligence, or irresponsibility. We made every call, submitted every application, followed up on every deadline, and did everything we were told to do — often doing the servicer's job for them. The VA and our mortgage servicer (Freedom Mortgage, a designated "repeat offender" with $130.3 million in federal penalties) put us into a cycle of programs that were cancelled, restarted, and stalled for years. None of it mattered, because Freedom Mortgage wasn't submitting the paperwork. This was confirmed on a recorded call.

What happened to our family is part of a documented, systemic failure — not something we caused or could have prevented. NPR, Congress, the VA Inspector General, and major veteran service organizations are all actively investigating or advocating on this crisis. For the full background, see the bottom of this page.

Resources & What We're Figuring Out

We're incredibly grateful for everyone who's reached out to help. We want to be transparent about where things stand with different options, because each one comes with real logistical questions we're still working through. None of this is to say we're not thankful — we absolutely are. We just want you to have the full picture so nobody worries about things that are already being figured out.

If we need to relocate
We're preparing for the possibility that we will need to move. We don't know the timeline yet — it could be weeks, could be longer. We'll ask for specific help when we know more. For now, just knowing you're in our corner means everything.
Vernon's property (Tyler/Cheney area)
Vernon has generously offered to let us put an RV or trailer on his property, which is huge. We're still working out the logistics — we'd need to build a fence to separate the dogs, and there aren't hookups, so electricity, water, shower/bath, and laundry are all things we'd have to figure out. But having a landing spot matters a lot.
Andrew's family — possible RV
Andrew's family may be able to help with a 27-foot RV/trailer. We're still finding out whether it's all-weather capable and whether there's enough sleeping space for our kiddo. More info to come.
SSVF (Supportive Services for Veteran Families)
This is the same program that helped us back in 2019. They've reached out again and said they can help with relocation costs, deposits, and finding housing if it comes to that. We used partial services last time, so we're confirming what they can actually do now vs. then, since the program has changed under the last two administrations.
HUD-VASH voucher
Our HUD-VASH case manager has been helpful, but we're not sure the voucher math works for us. Spokane Housing Authority may be using outdated eligibility rules that include Jerrod's disability income when they shouldn't, and it's been nearly impossible to find the actual 2-bedroom eligibility limits to confirm. A voucher capped at $1,300/month for a 2-bedroom in this market is tight, and the program comes with a lot of complexity. Still evaluating.
WA Dept of Veterans Affairs — VIP Program
WDVA has a Veterans Innovation Program that can provide $3,500 if we need to relocate. Every bit helps, and we're grateful this exists.
Spokane County utilities assistance
Leann needs to apply for utilities assistance to get that off our monthly expenses while we figure the rest out. This is on the to-do list.
Elizabeth Dole Foundation
EDF is our strongest institutional support right now. Meredith Beck (VP of Government Relations) is personally leading, met with the Deputy Secretary of VA this week, and is pushing hard for answers. EDF's Capitol Hill Day is April 16 and our case is on the agenda.
Wounded Warrior Project — Resource Center
Separate from WWP's government affairs response (which has been slow), the WWP Resource Center caseworker who called was genuinely helpful. She's getting us set up with a dedicated caseworker who can take over some of the coordination work that Leann has been carrying alone. Leann needs to check email and send her our info/docs to get that rolling.
Spokane County Veterans Center
Part of the VA referral process requires Jerrod to go in person to the Spokane County Veterans Center and sit down with a VSO (Veterans Service Officer) for about 30 minutes to evaluate options and document the situation. Jerrod is working on scheduling that. To be candid — the initial phone contact wasn't great (the person seemed more interested in checking a box for his boss than actually helping), but the in-person meeting is a required step, so he's doing it.
The VBA email chain — helpful and unhelpful
After the NPR article, someone inside the VA's Veterans Benefits Administration started an email chain that went through WA state and Spokane VA programs — but it didn't include the NPR article or accurate details about our situation. Result: a flood of calls from people who'd been told to help but didn't know what was going on. Most of them couldn't help with the foreclosure itself, but some connected us with programs above (SSVF, VIP, utilities). The silver lining is that dozens of VA insiders in Washington now understand what's actually happening.

Background

If you're new to this or want to understand how we got here, here's the full picture.

This isn't just about our family. Since the VA killed their rescue program (VASP) in May 2025, over 10,000 veteran families have already lost their homes. Another 90,000 are currently at risk of foreclosure right now. The total number of impacted families is over 160,000 and climbing. NPR, Congress, the VA Inspector General, and major veteran service organizations have all been investigating and advocating on this crisis.

Our mortgage servicer, Freedom Mortgage, has $130.3 million in cumulative federal penalties. They've been fined by the CFPB in 2019, 2023, and 2024. The Department of Justice hit them with a $113 million settlement for certifying loans that didn't meet standards. New Jersey's Attorney General issued a consent order for "bait-and-switch" refinancing targeting veterans. Washington State took enforcement action against Freedom Mortgage and its CEO personally in 2009. This is who the VA entrusted with our loan.

We were not asking for a handout. We were asking to pay our mortgage. We were not allowed to. Freedom Mortgage and the VA put us into a cycle of programs that were cancelled, restarted, and stalled — for years. We did everything we were told to do. We made every call, submitted every application, followed up on every deadline. None of it mattered, because Freedom Mortgage wasn't submitting the paperwork. This was confirmed on a recorded call.

We found out about the foreclosure when someone showed up at our door with the notice.

The worst part: Freedom Mortgage offered us a 7.25% interest rate on our 2.25% VA loan — that's more than triple our rate, and 400 basis points over the VA's own cap. They promised a VASP review in writing in January 2025, then marked every single option "Not Reviewed" 19 days later. The VA foreclosed while the loss mitigation application sat unsubmitted.

There are now nine documented regulatory violations in our case. There is an active congressional investigation. The VA Inspector General has an open federal investigation. We have the evidence, the documentation, and the support of multiple congressional offices and organizations. What we still don't have is our home secured — because the VA has the authority to fix this and won't.
